# Funding Opportunities for ASU Student Entrepreneurs

## Overview

ASU offers multiple funding pathways for student entrepreneurs, from small grants for validation to larger investments for scaling. This guide covers internal ASU funding, with notes on external opportunities.

---

## Internal ASU Funding

### 1. E+I Microgrants

**Amount:** $500 - $2,500
**Best For:** Early-stage customer discovery, prototyping, initial validation
**Eligibility:** Any enrolled ASU student with an active venture concept
**Timeline:** Rolling applications, typically 2-4 week decisions

**What They Fund:**
- Customer interview incentives
- Prototype materials
- Market research tools
- Minimal viable product development
- Travel for customer discovery

**What They Don't Fund:**
- Salaries or personal expenses
- Equipment that isn't venture-specific
- Marketing before validation

### 2. Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative

**Amount:** $5,000 - $20,000
**Best For:** Ventures with demonstrated traction and clear path to revenue
**Eligibility:** ASU students with existing ventures (not just ideas)
**Timeline:** Annual competition, typically Fall application

**Requirements:**
- Demonstrated customer validation
- Working prototype or MVP
- Clear business model
- Presentation to selection committee

**Selection Criteria:**
- Innovation and market potential
- Team capability and commitment
- Progress since inception
- Viability of business model

### 3. Venture Devils Demo Day Prizes

**Amount:** $2,500 - $15,000 (varies by cohort)
**Best For:** Cohort participants with investor-ready pitches
**Eligibility:** Must complete Venture Devils program
**Timeline:** End of each semester

### 4. ASU Innovation Open

**Amount:** Up to $50,000 in prizes
**Best For:** High-potential ventures across student and faculty categories
**Eligibility:** ASU students, faculty, and staff
**Timeline:** Annual competition (Spring)

**Categories:**
- Student ventures
- Faculty/staff ventures
- Social impact ventures

## External Funding Sources

### SBIR/STTR Grants (Federal)

**Amount:** $50,000 - $1,500,000+
**Best For:** Technology-based startups, especially with research component
**Eligibility:** US-based small businesses (must incorporate)

**Note:** ASU has dedicated support for SBIR/STTR through SkySong Innovations. They can help with:
- Application preparation
- Technical assistance
- Match funding in some cases

### Arizona Innovation Challenge

**Amount:** Up to $250,000
**Best For:** Technology ventures with significant Arizona economic impact
**Eligibility:** Arizona-based companies (can incorporate in AZ)

### Thiel Fellowship

**Amount:** $100,000 over two years
**Best For:** Founders under 23 willing to leave school to pursue ventures
**Note:** Highly competitive; requires dropping out

### Y Combinator, Techstars, etc.

**Amount:** Varies ($125,000+ for YC)
**Best For:** Ventures ready for intensive acceleration and fundraising
**Consideration:** May require relocation; significant equity dilution

## Non-Dilutive Funding Tips

### What is Non-Dilutive Funding?
Money that doesn't require giving up equity in your company. Includes:
- Grants
- Competitions
- Revenue from customers (the best kind!)

### Why Prioritize Non-Dilutive Early?
1. Preserves ownership
2. Validates without investor pressure
3. No repayment required
4. Can make you more attractive to investors later

### Best Practices
- Apply to multiple opportunities simultaneously
- Budget time for applications (they take effort)
- Track deadlines in a calendar
- Ask for feedback on unsuccessful applications
- Build relationships with program administrators

## Bootstrapping as a Strategy

Not every business needs funding. Consider bootstrapping if:
- Your business can generate early revenue
- You can build an MVP with minimal capital
- You want to maintain full control
- Your market doesn't require massive scale to win

### Bootstrapping Resources at ASU
- Free mentorship through E+I
- Free prototyping in maker spaces
- Student labor (internships, class projects)
- University facilities and resources
