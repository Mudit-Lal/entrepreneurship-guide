# Venture Devils

## Program Overview

Venture Devils is ASU's premier student startup accelerator program, designed to help student entrepreneurs launch and grow their ventures. The program provides mentorship, resources, funding opportunities, and culminates in Demo Day—a pitch competition where startups present to investors and the ASU community.

## Eligibility

- Must be a currently enrolled ASU student (undergraduate or graduate)
- Can be a team or solo founder
- Must have a venture concept (doesn't need to be fully developed)
- Open to all majors and colleges within ASU
- F-1 visa students can participate (but should consult OIS regarding any work/income considerations)

## Program Structure

### Cohort Experience
- **Duration:** One semester (Fall or Spring)
- **Commitment:** ~5-10 hours per week
- **Format:** Weekly workshops, mentor meetings, and peer sessions

### What You Get
1. **Mentorship:** Matched with experienced entrepreneurs and industry experts
2. **Curriculum:** Lean startup methodology, customer discovery, financial modeling, pitching
3. **Resources:** Access to prototyping labs, legal templates, market research tools
4. **Community:** Network of fellow student founders
5. **Funding Opportunity:** Demo Day prize money and investor connections

## Demo Day

Demo Day is the culminating event of each Venture Devils cohort. Teams pitch their ventures to:
- Angel investors and VCs
- ASU faculty and leadership
- Alumni entrepreneurs
- Community members

### Demo Day Prizes
- First Place: Typically $10,000-$15,000
- Second Place: Typically $5,000-$7,500
- Third Place: Typically $2,500-$5,000
- Additional category prizes available

### What Judges Look For
1. **Problem-Solution Fit:** Is there a real problem? Does your solution address it?
2. **Market Opportunity:** Is the market large enough? Can you capture it?
3. **Traction:** Have you talked to customers? Any early validation?
4. **Team:** Why are you the right people to build this?
5. **Business Model:** How will you make money? Do the unit economics work?
6. **Ask:** What do you need? How will you use the funding?

## How to Apply

1. Visit the E+I Institute website
2. Complete the Venture Devils application form
3. Include: Team info, venture description, problem statement, current stage
4. Applications typically open 2-4 weeks before semester start

## Tips for Success

### Before Applying
- Talk to at least 10 potential customers
- Have a clear problem statement
- Know your target customer
- Be coachable and open to pivoting

### During the Program
- Attend all sessions—the network is half the value
- Be honest with mentors about challenges
- Focus on customer discovery over building
- Practice your pitch weekly
- Help other teams—the community reciprocates

### For Demo Day
- Start practicing your pitch from week one
- Tell a story, don't just list features
- Show don't tell—demos beat slides
- Know your numbers cold
- Prepare for tough questions
