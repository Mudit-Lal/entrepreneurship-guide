# Technology Entrepreneurship and Management (TEM) Program

## Overview

The Technology Entrepreneurship and Management (TEM) program at ASU, also known as the Management of Technology (MOT) program, prepares students to lead innovation-driven ventures and organizations. It combines business acumen with technology understanding.

## Program Options

### Master of Science in Technology (Management of Technology)
- **Duration:** 1-2 years depending on course load
- **Format:** Evening classes (designed for working professionals)
- **Location:** Tempe campus + online options

### Graduate Certificate in Technology Entrepreneurship
- **Duration:** 1-2 semesters
- **Credits:** 15 credit hours
- **Focus:** Concentrated entrepreneurship curriculum

### Undergraduate Minor in Entrepreneurship
- **Available to:** Any ASU undergraduate
- **Credits:** 18 credit hours
- **Focus:** Foundational entrepreneurship skills

## Core Curriculum Areas

### Technology Strategy
- How technology creates competitive advantage
- Technology adoption and diffusion
- Platform and ecosystem strategies
- Intellectual property strategy

### Entrepreneurship
- Opportunity recognition
- Business model design
- Startup finance
- Scaling ventures

### Innovation Management
- R&D management
- Corporate innovation
- Open innovation
- Innovation metrics

### Leadership
- Leading technical teams
- Change management
- Organizational design for innovation

## Key Courses

### TMC 501 - Technology Strategy
Frameworks for technology-based competition and innovation leadership.

### TMC 502 - Technology Entrepreneurship
From idea to venture launch, including business planning and early-stage funding.

### TMC 503 - Innovation Management
Managing innovation within organizations, corporate entrepreneurship.

### TMC 504 - Technology Commercialization
Bringing new technologies to market, licensing, and startup formation.

### TMC 505 - Technology Venture Finance
Funding innovation from bootstrapping through exit.

## Who Should Consider TEM/MOT

### Good Fit If You:
- Have a technical background and want business skills
- Want to start a technology-based company
- Lead innovation efforts at an employer
- Are transitioning from technical to management roles
- Want to understand how to commercialize research

### May Not Be the Best Fit If:
- You want a traditional MBA
- You're focused on non-tech entrepreneurship
- You prefer daytime classes (most are evening)

## Integration with ASU Entrepreneurship Ecosystem

### Venture Devils
Many TEM students participate in Venture Devils to apply classroom learning.

### E+I Institute
Access to E+I resources, mentors, and funding opportunities.

### Capstone Projects
Many courses include real venture development projects.

### Industry Connections
Guest speakers, company visits, and networking with alumni entrepreneurs.

## Admission Requirements

### MS in Technology (MOT)
- Bachelor's degree from accredited institution
- Minimum GPA 3.0 in last 60 hours
- GMAT/GRE (may be waived with significant experience)
- Resume demonstrating professional experience
- Statement of purpose

### Graduate Certificate
- Bachelor's degree
- Lower GPA threshold than full MS
- Can often be completed alongside employment

## Career Outcomes

Graduates typically pursue:
- **Startup Founders:** Launch their own technology ventures
- **Corporate Innovation:** Lead R&D or new product development
- **Technology Consulting:** Advise on technology strategy
- **Venture Capital:** Evaluate and support tech investments
- **Technology Management:** CTO, VP Engineering, Product roles

## Tips for Prospective Students

1. **Talk to current students:** Get honest perspective on workload
2. **Have a venture idea:** You'll get more from the program
3. **Engage beyond class:** Join E+I activities, attend events
4. **Build relationships:** Your cohort becomes your network
5. **Apply learning immediately:** Real-world application cements concepts
