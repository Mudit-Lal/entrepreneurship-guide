# Entrepreneurship + Innovation (E+I) Institute

## Overview

The Entrepreneurship + Innovation (E+I) Institute at ASU is the central hub for entrepreneurship education, programming, and support across all campuses. It operates under the Office of Knowledge Enterprise and serves students, faculty, and community members.

## Key Programs

### Blackstone LaunchPad

A campus-based entrepreneurship program powered by the Blackstone Charitable Foundation.

**What It Offers:**
- One-on-one coaching from experienced entrepreneurs
- Workshops on startup fundamentals
- Access to Blackstone's global network
- Microgrant opportunities ($500-$2,500)
- Connection to investors and mentors

**Location:** Multiple campus locations including Tempe, Downtown Phoenix, Polytechnic, and West

**Who It's For:** Any ASU student with an entrepreneurial idea, regardless of major or stage

### Venture Devils (see separate document)

### Change the World Workshops

Free workshops open to all students covering:
- Business model canvas
- Customer discovery
- Financial modeling basics
- Pitch development
- Legal basics for startups

### Founder Fundamentals

An introductory course for aspiring entrepreneurs covering:
- Ideation and opportunity recognition
- Market research
- MVP development
- Team building
- Funding basics

## Funding Opportunities

### E+I Microgrants
- Amount: $500-$2,500
- For: Prototyping, customer research, MVP development
- Eligibility: Any ASU student with an active venture

### Edson Student Entrepreneur Initiative
- Amount: $5,000-$20,000
- For: High-potential student ventures
- More competitive; requires existing traction

### ASU Innovation Open
- Major annual competition
- Prizes up to $50,000
- Open to student and faculty ventures

## How to Get Involved

### Step 1: Attend an Event
- Drop-in hours at Blackstone LaunchPad
- Change the World workshops
- Networking events and pitch nights

### Step 2: Meet with a Coach
- Schedule a one-on-one meeting
- Free for all ASU students
- Discuss your idea and get guidance

### Step 3: Join a Program
- Apply for Venture Devils (cohort-based)
- Participate in competitions
- Access ongoing mentorship

## Tips for Working with E+I

1. **Start early:** Don't wait until you have a "perfect" idea
2. **Be consistent:** Regular engagement yields better results than one-off meetings
3. **Come prepared:** Have specific questions, not just "I need help"
4. **Follow up:** After meetings, send a summary and next steps
5. **Give back:** Help other founders once you've learned
